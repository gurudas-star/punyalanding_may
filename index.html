Don't Ignore These!

Many women assume heavy periods and pelvic pain are "normal." Ignoring them may lead to:

  • Severe anemia
  • Increasing abdominal enlargement
  • Chronic pelvic discomfort
  • Fertility difficulties

Symptoms

You may require fibroid treatment if you experience

  • Heavy or prolonged menstrual bleeding
  • Bleeding between periods
  • Pelvic pain or pressure
  • Abdominal bloating or enlargement
  • Frequent urination
  • Lower back pain
  • Pain during intercourse
  • Constipation or bowel problems
  • Fatigue due to anemia
  • Difficulty conceiving

People Also Ask

Frequently Asked Questions

Are fibroids cancerous?

Fibroids are usually non-cancerous and rarely become cancerous.

Can fibroids affect fertility?

Yes, depending on their size and location.

Do all fibroids require surgery?

No. Many fibroids can be managed with observation or medication.

Can fibroids grow over time?

Yes, some fibroids may gradually increase in size.

Can fibroids return after treatment?

Some fibroids may recur, especially in younger women.

Is laparoscopic fibroid surgery safe?

Yes, minimally invasive surgery is widely used and offers faster recovery in suitable patients.

Can fibroids cause abdominal enlargement?

Yes, larger fibroids can make the abdomen appear enlarged.

How are fibroids diagnosed?

Fibroids are usually diagnosed through examination and ultrasound scanning.

Will fibroid treatment affect pregnancy?

Many treatments are planned specifically to preserve fertility and improve pregnancy outcomes.

When should I see a gynecologist for fibroids?

If you have heavy periods, pelvic pain, bloating, or fertility concerns, consultation is recommended.
